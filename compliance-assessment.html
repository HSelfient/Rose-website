Section 3 · Employment & Hiring
Deep Dive 05 / 19
Have you verified that none of your HR technology vendors use automated scoring or ranking features that would constitute an Automated Employment Decision Tool under NYC Local Law 144?
Platforms such as Workday, Greenhouse, HireVue, LinkedIn Recruiter, and many others include AI scoring features that may be active by default. The deploying organization — not the vendor — bears LL144 responsibility.
Section 4 · Transparency & Consumer Rights
Deep Dive 06 / 19
When AI is used in decisions that materially affect individuals, do you provide clear notice and offer a mechanism to appeal or seek human review?
Required across NYC, Colorado, EU AI Act, California, and Texas. The right to contest or seek correction of an AI-influenced decision is increasingly a legal requirement, not a courtesy.
Section 4 · Transparency
Deep Dive 07 / 19
When your AI systems interact directly with end users or customers, do they disclose that the interaction is AI-generated or AI-mediated?
EU AI Act Article 50 mandates disclosure for all AI systems that interact with natural persons. This applies to chatbots, virtual agents, AI-generated content, and any synthetic output presented to users.
Section 4 · Generative & Frontier AI
Deep Dive 08 / 19
Does your organization develop, fine-tune, or operate large-scale generative AI or foundation models — including LLMs, image generators, or video synthesis systems — for use by others?
California SB 53 and AB 2013 require safety and governance frameworks and training data provenance disclosure. South Korea's AI Basic Act imposes high-impact AI reporting. EU AI Act Title VIII targets General Purpose AI model providers.
Section 4 · EU AI Act Obligations
Deep Dive 09 / 19
For any high-risk AI systems deployed in EU markets, has your organization completed — or initiated — the required conformity assessment and registered the system in the EU AI database?
Full high-risk AI obligations apply from August 2026. Organizations that have not yet mapped their systems against Annex III risk inadvertent non-compliance as the deadline approaches.
Section 5 · Logging & Proof
Deep Dive 10 / 19
How does your organization currently maintain proof of AI decision-making for audit or regulatory purposes?
EU AI Act Articles 12 and 26 require automatic logging of high-risk AI decision events. Colorado impact assessments depend on accurate execution records. NYC bias audits require reconstructable decision histories.
Section 5 · Logging & Proof
Deep Dive 11 / 19
Do your AI execution records capture the specific rules, model version, input data, confidence scores, and governing policies in force at the precise moment each decision was made?
A log entry stating "candidate rejected" is not the same as a non-repudiable execution record. Regulators and courts will test whether execution evidence can withstand adversarial challenge.
Section 5 · Incident Response
Deep Dive 12 / 19
Does your organization have a documented AI incident response protocol — covering detection, internal escalation, and regulatory notification — for bias events, errors, or harmful AI outputs?
EU AI Act Article 73 requires mandatory reporting of serious incidents to market surveillance authorities. Colorado mandates reporting within 90 days. A generic IT incident process does not satisfy AI-specific requirements.
Section 5 · Human Oversight
Deep Dive 13 / 19
For high-risk AI systems, are human operators technically capable of understanding, questioning, and overriding AI decisions in real time?
EU AI Act Article 14 requires that oversight be genuine — not merely nominal. A human who cannot meaningfully interpret or challenge a decision does not constitute lawful oversight under the Act's standard.

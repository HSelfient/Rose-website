AI Governance
by Jurisdiction

Current standing as of Q1 2026 — subject to ongoing legislative developments

In Force European Union

The EU AI Act (Regulation 2024/1689) is the world's most comprehensive AI governance framework. Phased implementation began February 2025. Full high-risk AI obligations apply from August 2026. Fines reach €35M or 7% of global annual turnover.

Key obligation: Non-repudiable proof of execution for all high-risk AI decisions.

Evolving United States

Federal AI legislation remains fragmented. State-level legislation is accelerating: New York's Local Law 144 mandates bias audits for AI hiring tools. Colorado, Illinois, and California have enacted or are advancing AI-specific statutes.

Key risk: State-level patchwork compliance requirements for AI in employment decisions.

Developing United Kingdom

The UK has adopted a principles-based, sector-led approach. The AI Safety Institute operates as a global evaluation body. Sector regulators — FCA, ICO, CMA — are issuing AI-specific guidance within existing frameworks.

Key risk: Regulatory uncertainty; sector-specific obligations diverging from EU standards.

Advancing Canada

Bill C-27, including AIDA, has advanced through Parliament. AIDA would impose requirements on high-impact AI systems, including impact assessments, human oversight, and transparency obligations broadly aligned with the EU AI Act.

Key obligation: Impact assessments and human oversight for high-impact AI systems.

Voluntary Singapore

Singapore's Model AI Governance Framework and AI Verify toolkit provide voluntary guidance. MAS has issued specific guidance on AI use in financial services. Singapore positions itself as a pro-innovation AI hub while signalling binding requirements may follow.

Key focus: Voluntary governance with strong regulatory engagement in financial services.

Advancing Brazil

Brazil's AI Bill (PL 2338/2023), modelled significantly on the EU AI Act, passed the Senate in 2024 and is advancing in the Chamber of Deputies. Brazil is positioned to become the most EU-aligned AI regulatory regime outside Europe.

Key alignment: Closely mirrors EU AI Act risk tiers and high-risk category definitions.

Consulting Australia

Australia has published an Interim Response to its AI Safety consultation and is developing a mandatory guardrails framework for high-risk AI. Comprehensive legislation is expected within the current parliamentary cycle.

Key signal: Moving from voluntary guidance toward mandatory guardrails for high-risk AI.

Principled Japan

Japan's approach emphasises agile, principles-based guidelines rather than prescriptive legislation. METI and the Cabinet Office have published AI governance guidelines aligned with the OECD AI Principles. Binding sector-specific requirements are emerging.

Key stance: Innovation-first with sector-specific binding requirements emerging.

Organizations operating across multiple jurisdictions face compounding compliance obligations. ROSÉ's execution proof layer satisfies the most demanding regime — meaning compliance with the EU AI Act typically satisfies the requirements of all others simultaneously.

The Black-Box AI Problem
You Likely Have

AI agents are already making decisions that can cost companies money, trigger liability, and change human lives. Most enterprises still cannot prove what those systems did. That is the real black-box problem.

Agentic AI changes the fundamental bargain of enterprise software. AI is no longer just generating text or assisting employees. It is starting to screen candidates, move money, route claims, trigger approvals, modify records, and coordinate actions across multiple systems. Those actions are increasingly consequential.

Can you prove what happened? Not just to an engineer. Not just to an internal review team. But to a regulator, a court, an insurer, an auditor, or a board. That is a very different standard.

ROSÉ was built to solve this problem. It exists at the point where AI-mediated activity becomes economically, legally, or operationally significant — creating governed, ordered, timestamped, cryptographically sealed proof records for consequential execution.
